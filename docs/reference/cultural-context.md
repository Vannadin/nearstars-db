# Cultural context — NS systems in science fiction

NearStars covers real nearby stars; several of those have prominent appearances in science fiction. This doc indexes the connections, distinguishes novel-fact from real-fact, and flags retracted-or-refuted planets that fiction has anchored to. Cultural references are NOT cfg-emitted — keep this doc as narrative reference only.

## Tau Ceti — *Project Hail Mary* (Andy Weir, 2021)

- **Body in novel**: "Adrian" = Tau Cet e
- **Status in real world**: Tau Cet e was reclassified to **"False Positive Planet"** by NASA Exoplanet Archive on 2026-04-09, citing Figueira et al. 2025 (`2025A&A...700A.174F`) ESPRESSO sub-10 cm/s RV non-detection. The novel was written from the Feng 2017 detection picture; that picture is now obsolete.
- **What the novel got right**: G8V spectral type, ~9 Gyr age (twice solar), Tau Ceti as a long-stable astrobiology target
- **What the novel got wrong (per current data)**: Adrian as a habitable super-Earth — the underlying RV signal is now considered stellar activity. The novel also omits the substantial debris disk (ALMA-resolved 6–55 AU, ~1.2 M⊕ dust, MacGregor 2016), which would pose a real navigation hazard.
- **Weir's stated Adrian parameters** (compiled from novel text + Weir interviews; see `docs/phase3/tau-cet-e.md § Bibliography`): surface gravity **1.4 g** (Weir Sciam podcast; from Feng 2017's 3.93 M⊕ + rocky mass-radius relation, implying ~1.68 R⊕); atmosphere **91% CO₂ / 7% CH₄ / 1% Ar / trace** (no significant N₂ — Taumoeba is nitrogen-intolerant); surface pressure not stated, but 0.02 bar / −51 °C at 91.2 km altitude implies a multi-bar CO₂-dominated surface; color **green** from space (photochemical haze + airborne Taumoeba biology); Weir uses Feng 2017's orbit directly (P ≈ 168 d, a ≈ 0.55 AU); day length / rotation not stated; **no surface ocean** — the sampling mission targets upper-atmosphere clouds at 91.2 km via a 10 km xenonite chain, never the surface.
- **Diverges from the "habitable Earth-analog" framing**: Weir's Adrian is *not* a wet Earth-analog. It is a CO₂-dominated world with photochemical CH₄ haze and an airborne microbial ecosystem (Taumoeba lives in the upper atmosphere). The "habitable super-Earth" reading was a 2017–2024 popular-science gloss on Feng's numbers, not Weir's depiction. The 1.4 g surface gravity (rather than the catalog 1.20 g) also implies Weir adopted a rockier, smaller-radius body than the popular-science sub-Neptune fit.
- **Adrian as the unique Astrophage-resistant biosphere**: the novel's central worldbuilding premise. Astrophage seeded Earth, Erid, and Adrian from a single Adrian-origin panspermia event (Weir, Astronomy.com interview); only Adrian carries Taumoeba, the natural predator, which is why Astrophage has out-grown its predator in every other system. NS cannot model this in cfg — flavor-text only.
- **Adrian ≠ Erid (worldbuilding-conflation warning)**: Adrian (Tau Cet e) is 1.4 g + CO₂-dominated. Erid (40 Eri A b), per Weir's worldbuilding companion document, is **2.09 g** + **28 atm NH₃** (made-up but not in conflict with observation) + **5.1-h sidereal day** (≈ 18 397 s) + **210 °C** surface temperature (boiling point of water raised by pressure → liquid water still present) + **25 × Earth** magnetic field + **8.47 M⊕** (Ma 2018 measured) + **12 835 km** radius (Weir-derived). Popular summaries (including some search-engine answer boxes) routinely conflate the two worlds. Anchor parameters above are Adrian-specific; for Erid see the 40 Eri A entry below.
- **NS DB**: e (= Adrian) was a NEA False Positive and absent, but is now **re-included per project decision (2026-06-03)** at the pre-retraction Feng 2017 values, flagged refuted in Phase 3 (`docs/phase3/tau-cet-e.md`, documented-divergence framing) — the PHM parallel to 40 Eri b (Erid). f/g/h remain `pl_controv_flag=1` but on retraction watch (Figueira 2025 also reports them at or below detection floor).

## 40 Eridani A — *Star Trek* (Vulcan) + *Project Hail Mary* (Erid)

- **Body in fiction**: 40 Eri A b serves as Vulcan in the *Star Trek* canon (Roddenberry's letter to *Sky & Telescope* 1991 confirmed 40 Eri over Epsilon Eri). The Eridians of *Project Hail Mary* (Rocky's species) also originate from 40 Eridani A.
- **Status in real world**: announced by Ma 2018 (`2018AJ....155..117M`; 42.245 d, 8.47 M⊕ super-Earth, a ≈ 0.224 AU, e = 0.04) but **refuted by Burrows/Lubin 2024** — the 42-day signal is the stellar rotation period, not a Keplerian planet. **Re-included in the NS DB per project decision (2026-06-03)** at the pre-retraction Ma 2018 values, flagged refuted in Phase 3 (cultural weight: Vulcan + Erid). Not in the habitable zone in modern terms; the Vulcan fiction predates the discovery and assumed an Earth-like world.
- **Weir's stated Erid parameters** (companion worldbuilding document, "eridian.docx" provided by user 2026-05-27): real-observation values are mass 8.47 M⊕, semi-major axis 0.224 AU, e = 0.04, period 42.245 d, stellar flux 12 537 W/m². Made-up parameters consistent with observation: composition rocky-metal-rich, density 5 710 kg/m³, radius 12 835 km, atmosphere mostly NH₃ (Weir writes "NH4" — chemical-formula typo for NH₃), surface pressure 28 atm, mean surface temperature 210 °C, derived gravity 2.09 g, magnetic field ≈ 25 × Earth (required to retain such a thick ammonia atmosphere), molten metal core, tectonic activity, liquid water present (raised boiling point of water above 210 °C at 28 atm), solar day 5.1 h (≈ 18 397 s, fast enough to dynamo-generate the field).
- **Weir's panspermia framing**: the worldbuilding companion states "A panspermia event from Tau Ceti billions of years ago seeded life on both Earth and the Eridian homeworld." This sets Adrian (Tau Cet e) as the panspermia origin and Earth + Erid as recipients — consistent with the Astrophage backstory in the novel and with the Adrian section above. NS does not model this; flavor-text only.
- **Project Hail Mary (2026 film)**: the film depicts Erid with a **deep-blue atmosphere and a ring system**. This is the film's art direction — it diverges from Weir's worldbuilding (a colourless ~28 atm NH₃ atmosphere, no rings) and is **fiction, not a real attribute of 40 Eri A**. Recorded as cultural reference only; the DB carries only the real Ma 2018 RV candidate (mass + orbit), never a fictional atmosphere or rings.
- **NS DB**: `40_eridani_a.json` (host A, with planet b **re-included as a refuted-flagged documented divergence per user decision 2026-06-03**) + `40_eridani_b.json` (white dwarf, separate star) + `40_eridani_c.json` (M dwarf) — multi-star system. Phase 3 (`docs/phase3/40-eridani-a.md`) documents the Burrows 2024 refutation.
- **Eridian cosmology (PHM, flavor-only)**: Erid's **dense atmosphere blocks light** and its **strong magnetic field blocks radiation**, so Eridians evolved **blind**, sensing by **echolocation** (an audio/frequency "whalesong + sonar" language; Rocky perceives 360° without blind spots). Consequently their astronomy is very late — they **discovered neighboring planets only recently** and lacked concepts like radioactivity and special relativity (the relativity gap nearly wrecks their Erid→Tau Ceti voyage). NS does not model this (flavor-text only), but it **cross-validates Weir's Erid parameters** above: the blindness *requires* exactly the opaque thick-NH₃ atmosphere + very strong (≈25×Earth) magnetic field of the worldbuilding doc. Sources: PHM Wiki (Eridian), Wikipedia (Project Hail Mary).

## Epsilon Eridani — *Babylon 5* (Vorlon homeworld), early *Star Trek* (original Vulcan)

- **Body in fiction**: Vorlon Prime in *Babylon 5* (J. Michael Straczynski). Original Vulcan in early *Star Trek* lore before being retconned to 40 Eri A.
- **Status in real world**: ε Eri b confirmed RV planet (Hatzes 2000 + Mawet 2019 direct imaging) at ~3.5 AU, M sin i ~0.78 M_Jup — a gas giant, not Earth-like. Surrounded by a three-belt debris disk.
- **NS DB**: `eps_eri.json` (host + b in DB; Phase 3 synthesis at `docs/phase3/eps-eri.md` + `eps-eri-b.md`)

## Wolf 359 — *Star Trek: The Next Generation* (Battle of Wolf 359)

- **Body in fiction**: The Battle of Wolf 359 (TNG "The Best of Both Worlds", 1990) is a fictional Starfleet encounter with the Borg — set at the real star's location but with no canonical planet.
- **Status in real world**: Wolf 359 is an M6.5 flare star at 7.86 ly. No confirmed planets (deep RV upper limits).
- **NS DB**: `wolf_359.json` (host only, no planets)

## Alpha Centauri — *Avatar* (Pandora), *Three-Body Problem*, many

- **Body in fiction**: Pandora orbiting α Cen A in *Avatar* (Cameron 2009). The trisolaran homeworld of Liu Cixin's *Three-Body Problem* (the trisolaran problem is the canonical α Cen 3-body chaos).
- **Status in real world**: α Cen A/B with Proxima at ~13 000 AU. Proxima b confirmed habitable-zone Earth-mass; Proxima d candidate (Faria 2022). α Cen A/B planets remain unconfirmed (Beichman & Sanghi 2025 "S1" JWST/MIRI candidate at ~1.5 AU pending follow-up).
- **NS DB**: full triple system with Jacobi multi-star pipeline + Principia n-body export — the canonical hierarchical-multi-star showcase in NS

## Methodology note

Fiction does not override observation. When a novel anchors plot to a planet that is later refuted (like Tau Cet e), the NS DB follows real-world disposition. Cultural references are documented here as a narrative companion, not as cfg-emitted descriptions. If a future feature ships per-body description strings, this doc is the source of the fiction-aware flavor text — until then it is repo documentation only.
