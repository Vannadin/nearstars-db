# NearStars — mod title (Latin motto) candidates

Brainstorm record (2026-06-01; moved into docs/reference 2026-06-11).
All 8 candidates below are grammar-checked and verified unclaimed as mottos.
**Decision: deferred until release prep.** Two finalists are live.

## Finalists (the last two standing)

| Phrase | Literal | Closing note / texture |
|---|---|---|
| **Per vasta ad infinitum** | Across the vast void, toward infinity | Closes soaring into *infinity*. Vast + infinite share the same "immense emptiness" register — grand but slightly homogeneous |
| **Per aspera ad mira** | Through hardships, toward wonders | Closes on *wonder* (emotion). A hardship→wonder contrast arc; `mira` sounds clean with no stray associations |

## Earlier candidates

| Phrase | Literal | Closing note | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ad astra, et ultra** | To the stars, and beyond | Closes on *beyond* | Early candidate; shelved — `ultra` felt childish |
| **Ad infinitum, et ultra** | To infinity, and beyond | Closes on *beyond* | The Buzz Lightyear line in Latin; same `ultra` issue |
| **Per aspera ad infinitum** | Through hardships, toward infinity | Closes on *infinity* | The classic skeleton (= ad astra per aspera). But `ad infinitum` can read as "endless toil" |
| **Ad infinitum per aspera** | Toward infinity, through hardships | Closes on *hardship* | Word-order inversion of the above. Resolute, stoic tone; more motto-like |
| **Plus ultra ad infinitum** | Further beyond, toward infinity | Closes on *infinity* | Aggressive. But beyond+infinity is tautological + evokes My Hero Academia |
| **Per aspera plus ultra** | Through hardships, further beyond | Closes on *beyond* | Fusion of two real mottos, strongest rhythm. But zero celestial vocabulary |

## Grouped by closing flavor

- **Closing on infinity** (grand, cosmic) → Per vasta ad infinitum / Per aspera ad infinitum / Plus ultra ad infinitum
- **Closing on wonder** (emotional, sublime) → Per aspera ad mira
- **Closing on beyond** (momentum, breakthrough) → Per aspera plus ultra / Ad astra et ultra
- **Closing on hardship** (resolute, stoic) → Ad infinitum per aspera

## Reference — preempted mottos (must not be used as-is)

- Per aspera ad astra — state motto of Kansas
- Per ardua ad astra — Royal Air Force motto
- Plus ultra (alone) — national motto of Spain + My Hero Academia
- Per tenebras ad lucem — used by various knightly orders

(All 8 candidates above steer clear of these.)

## Parts palette (for further variations)

- Path `per` + accusative: aspera (hardships) / ardua (steeps)⚠ / tenebras (darkness) / noctem (night) / ignes (fires) / inane (the void)⭐ / umbras (shadows) / silentium (silence)⭐ / vasta (the vast)
- Destination `ad` + accusative: astra (stars)⚠ / sidera (stars, poetic) / caelum (sky) / aethera (the heavens) / lucem (light) / infinitum (infinity) / mira (wonders)⭐ / arcana (mysteries)⭐ / mundos (worlds)⭐ / orbes (celestial bodies) / ultima (the ends)
- Momentum adverbs: plus ultra (further beyond)⚠ / ulterius (farther) / ulteriusque (and farther) / semper ultra (ever beyond) / in aeternum (forever)
