# HD 69830 — Phase 3 Synthesis

HD 69830 (HIP 40693, GJ 9223) is a quiet old K0V star sitting at
the G8V/K0V spectral boundary, 12.58 ± 0.01 pc (41.05 ± 0.02 ly)
from the Sun via Gaia DR3 parallax 79.495 ± 0.040 mas. At V = 5.95
it sits just at the naked-eye threshold in Puppis. The star is
slightly less massive than Sol (0.863 ± 0.043 M☉, Tanner 2015
isochrone + bolometric-L Stefan-Boltzmann) and slightly smaller
(0.905 ± 0.019 R☉, same fit), at Teff = 5402 ± 11 K (Tanner 2015
high-resolution spectroscopy; Brewer 2016 SPOCS gives 5410 ± 25 K
in agreement), and modestly metal-poor at [Fe/H] = −0.04 ± 0.02
(Tanner 2015; Brewer 2016 gives −0.03 ± 0.04, Tsantaki 2013 gives
−0.06 ± 0.02 — all within combined errors). The 35.5 ± 0.5 day
rotation period (Suárez Mascareño 2015, from Ca II H&K activity
modulation in long-term HARPS spectra) is consistent with the
Mamajek & Hillenbrand 2008 gyrochronological 34 ± 3 d, and the
inferred age 5.7 ± 1 Gyr places HD 69830 slightly older than Sol
and squarely in the inactive late-G / early-K dwarf quiescent locus
(log R'HK = −4.95 ± 0.04, Gomes da Silva 2021 weighted mean of 240
HARPS spectra).

The system's defining feature is not the star itself but a
**warm dust population at ~1 AU** detected by Spitzer/IRS
(Beichman 2005): an unusual asteroid-belt analog interior to the
star's habitable zone, with strong 9.7 / 11.3 / 23 μm features
unambiguously identifying small (~μm) crystalline olivine grains.
Three Neptune-mass radial-velocity planets discovered by Lovis
2006 (b at 0.08 AU / 8.7 d, c at 0.19 AU / 31.6 d, d at 0.63 AU /
197 d) all orbit interior to the warm dust belt, with d sitting
just inside the belt's inner shoulder at ~0.9 AU (Smith, Wyatt &
Haniff 2009 mid-IR interferometry + SED refit: 0.92–1.16 AU).
d's 2:1 and 5:2 outer mean-motion resonances at 1.00 and 1.16 AU
land squarely within the belt, making d the dynamical sculptor of
the inner edge and the plausible parent-body source under the
Lisse 2007 catastrophic-event hypothesis. Lisse 2007
interpret the olivine-rich, sub-μm-dominated grain population as
post-collisional debris from a recent (≲ 1 Myr) catastrophic
disruption of a dwarf-planet-class body, citing Wyatt 2007's
steady-state collisional limit which forbids long-lived dust at
the observed luminosity (L_IR/L_★ ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁴) without an
implausibly massive parent reservoir. No cold (FIR) Vega-class
outer belt is detected at Spitzer 70 μm, ruling out a contributing
Kuiper-analog source.

**Scenario choice for NearStars: a quiet, slightly-redder-than-Sol
G8V star hosting an unusual close-in warm-dust ring at the orbit
of planet d's inner shoulder, with three Neptune-mass planets
flanking the dust.** The 18 cfg picks track canonical stellar
parameters where literature is settled; the disk geometry uses
the SED-inferred warm-belt scenario, and two tie-break picks
involve the visual hex tints (slightly redder-than-Sol cream;
warm amber dust glow). The catastrophic-event vs steady-state
origin is preserved in `## Canonical alternatives` since the cfg
optionally animates a "fresh debris" bright transient consistent
with Lisse 2007's interpretation but not strictly required.

## Surface synthesis

The photosphere of HD 69830 sits at the G–K boundary — Houk &
Smith-Moore 1988 catalogue it as K0V, while modern Teff scales
(Casagrande 2011 IRFM 5436 K, Tanner 2015 high-resolution
spectroscopy 5402 K, Brewer 2016 SPOCS 5410 K, Tsantaki 2013
homogeneous 5391 K) all cluster within 20 K of the G8V/K0V
transition near 5400 K. The letter-class assignment depends on
which calibration the catalog uses; the Phase 2 anchor Tanner
2019 (a paper dedicated to HD 69830) gives the tightest formal
uncertainty at 5402 ± 11 K, with the photosphere ~370 K cooler
than Sol. The 0.905 R☉ envelope (Tanner 2015 Stefan-Boltzmann
from bolometric L + Teff) gives 62% solar bolometric luminosity.
The metal-poor mild deficit ([Fe/H] = −0.04, Tanner 2015; −0.03
to −0.06 across Brewer 2016 / Tsantaki 2013 / Sousa 2008 / Beichman
2005) is a marginal departure from solar — within the
disk-population kinematic distribution and inconsequential for
opacity at this Teff.

No direct interferometric angular diameter measurement is
published — Boyajian 2012's CHARA survey did not include HD 69830,
and no VLTI-PIONIER programme through 2026 has measured the disk.
Tanner 2015's radius derives from Stefan-Boltzmann with the
self-consistent FEROS + HARPS Teff and the Spitzer / Hipparcos
bolometric flux; Rosenthal 2021's California Legacy Survey
isochrone fit (0.887 ± 0.019 R☉) and Stassun 2018 TIC-8 SED
(1.056 ± 0.126 R☉) both agree within combined errors. The cfg
`limb_darkening_alpha_h` is a 1D-atmosphere estimate at
α(H) ≈ 0.16, slightly stronger than Sol's 0.14 at the cooler
photosphere, consistent with the Claret–Bloemen 2011 grid for
log g ≈ 4.5, [Fe/H] = 0, Teff ≈ 5400 K. Granulation patterns are
expected to be Sun-like but ~5–10% smaller in linear scale per
the STAGGER grid scaling (Magic 2013): cells ~1000 km wide
instead of Sol's ~1300 km, with slightly longer lifetimes from
the cooler convective overturn.

Spot coverage is modest. log R'HK = −4.95 ± 0.04 (Gomes da Silva
2021 weighted mean of 240 HARPS spectra) places HD 69830 a tick
quieter than the modern Sun and squarely in the inactive
late-G / early-K dwarf locus; Lovis 2006 §4 explicitly notes
that the RV stability across the 4-year HARPS baseline used to
detect the three Neptune-mass planets is consistent with a star
at activity minimum throughout the campaign. Any cycle, if
present, is below the Lovis 2006 detection sensitivity (~5 m/s
amplitude). The cfg adopts a quiet-Sun spot envelope (~0.1% of
disk at notional cycle maximum, ~10× lower than Sol's
solar-maximum coverage) and renders no active-region structure.

The slightly metal-poor SED reddens the bolometric continuum
toward the blue by ~5 K equivalent versus a +0.0 dex reference,
imperceptible in the rendered hex; the dominant visible color
shift from Sol comes purely from the 370 K Teff deficit. The
visual cream tint `#ffe8c8` interpolates between Sol's `#fff8f0`
and α Cen B's `#ffcfa0` — about 2/3 of the way from Sol to B in
both the red-green and red-blue color differences.

## Atmosphere synthesis

HD 69830 hosts a quiet late-G / early-K chromosphere–transition-
region–corona structure typical of an old inactive dwarf. The
Ca II H&K chromospheric index log R'HK = −4.95 ± 0.04 (Gomes da
Silva 2021 HARPS weighted mean of 240 spectra over 2003–2018;
Boro Saikia 2018 multi-survey −4.99 and Henry 1996 Mt Wilson
single-snapshot −5.00 both agree) places it in the lower decile
of nearby G/K-dwarf activity. No long-baseline activity cycle
has been published — neither the Mt Wilson survey nor the HARPS
RV campaign saw a cycle period at >5 m/s amplitude — but for an
old quiet K0V a Sun-like 10–15 year cycle is the prior
expectation, just below detection. The cfg does not animate a
cycle.

No published X-ray detection. Schmitt 2004's ROSAT all-sky survey
catalogue does not list HD 69830 above the 5 × 10²⁶ erg/s
sensitivity floor at 12.6 pc, implying log L_X < 27.0 cgs.
Chandra and XMM-Newton did not target it; the most recent eROSITA
catalogue (Merloni 2024) places HD 69830 at log L_X ≈ 26.7
(0.2–2 keV; tentative low-S/N detection). The cfg adopts a
log L_X range 26.5–27.0 spanning Sun-quiet to Sun-cycle-max
analog levels — a tie-break in the absence of a measured cycle
amplitude.

Transition-region UV lines are not directly monitored; an old
G8V with log R'HK = −4.95 produces a Lyman-α continuum + Lyα line
roughly 50% of Sol's at the same heliocentric distance scaling.
Integrated XUV luminosity stays below 10⁻⁴ L_bol, far too feeble
to materially erode an Earth-equivalent atmosphere over Gyr
timescales at the star's habitable zone (~0.7 AU for HD 69830).
For the three Neptune-mass planets b/c/d the picture is
different — but those follow in their respective Phase 3
syntheses.

The corona/chromosphere visual extent is rendered at the
quiet-Sun reference — a thin chromospheric layer at ~1.005 R★
and a faint corona extending to ~3 R★ visible only during the
in-game "corona-on" rendering mode.

## Rotation & spin synthesis

The 35.5 ± 0.5 day rotation period from Suárez Mascareño 2015
is the only direct measurement, derived from rotational
modulation of Ca II H&K chromospheric activity in long-term
HARPS spectra (HD 69830 is too quiet for photometric P_rot at
HARPS or MOST continuum precision). The Mamajek & Hillenbrand
2008 gyrochronological estimate of 34 ± 3 d from log R'HK
calibration is consistent within 1σ. Both are in line with the
Skumanich P_rot ∝ √t expectation: Sol-calibrated gives ~30 d at
5 Gyr, scaling to ~35 d at 5.7 Gyr.

No asteroseismic detection — HD 69830 is below the asteroseismic
detection threshold of CHEOPS and TESS short-cadence
observations, and the predicted ν_max ≈ 3000 μHz with Δν ≈
130 μHz peaks too high in frequency for the survey cadences
deployed to date. The cfg adopts no oscillation-driven brightness
modulation.

Differential rotation is expected Sun-like with the equator
rotating ~20% faster than the poles, but unmeasured. The rotation
axis inclination is unconstrained — a randomly oriented axis is
adopted for visual rendering, tilted ~30° to the inferred
mean orbital plane of the b/c/d planet system.

In KSP units, 35.5 d × 86400 = **3 067 200 s** rotation period
for the star body, which is informational only (Kopernicus does
not animate stellar rotation directly, but the cfg
`rotationPeriod` field is read by Principia for spin-axis
precession bookkeeping).

## Visual styling

- **Orbit view (system-overview render).** A warm yellow-cream
  point against the warm-amber dust glow at ~1 AU, with three
  blue-green Neptune-mass planets visible at b ≈ 0.08 AU
  (innermost, lost in the stellar glare), c ≈ 0.19 AU (well inside
  the dust ring's inner edge), and d ≈ 0.63 AU (just interior to
  the dust ring's inner shoulder at ~0.9 AU, with d's 2:1 and 5:2
  outer MMRs landing inside the belt).
- **Stellar disk (close-up).** Quiet G8V with the textbook H-band
  power-law limb darkening; granulation cells slightly smaller
  than Sol's, slightly longer-lived; no active regions visible
  except as a faint cycle-averaged spot brightness modulation
  (~0.1% peak-to-peak) at the cfg's notional cycle maximum.
- **Stellar color.** Cream-yellow `#ffe8c8`, distinguishable
  from both the strict-solar `#fff8f0` and α Cen B's stronger
  `#ffcfa0` orange — HD 69830 is the "Sol's slightly redder
  cousin" entry in the NS catalog.
- **Warm dust ring.** This is the *defining* visual feature.
  The cfg renders a narrow `#d4a060` warm amber belt from 0.8
  to 1.5 AU with alpha 0.25 — substantially exaggerated above
  the true L_IR/L_★ = 2 × 10⁻⁴ (which would be invisible) to
  give the player something to look at. The ring is sub-micron
  olivine, predominantly scattering G8V starlight with a mild
  forward-scattering anisotropy (g ≈ 0.7) that brightens the
  morning/evening crescent as viewed from a hypothetical b/c/d
  planet. Document the dust amplification as a tie-break.
- **Catastrophic-event hypothesis cfg variant.** Per Lisse 2007,
  if the olivine grain population is post-collisional debris from
  a dwarf-planet disruption ~ 1 Myr ago, a bright transient
  asymmetric feature ("fresh debris cloud") could be modeled at a
  fixed orbital longitude with a brighter alpha (~0.5) and a
  ~10⁵ year decay timescale. The cfg ships the steady-state
  symmetric belt as the default and the catastrophic-debris
  asymmetric variant as a cfg alternative — both within the
  observation window.
- **Three Neptune-mass planets in sky.** Planet b at 0.08 AU
  shows a 0.04° apparent diameter from c (0.19 AU); planet d
  at 0.63 AU shows a similar 0.01° from c. From a hypothetical
  observer at b/c/d, the dust ring fills the orbital plane as a
  faint amber band — comparable to a moderately bright zodiacal
  light viewed from Earth, but at the dust belt's actual
  geometry. Planet-specific renders deferred to the b/c/d Phase
  3 syntheses.
- **Star in sky from Earth.** V = 5.95 places HD 69830 at the
  naked-eye threshold; in dark-sky conditions it is just visible
  in Puppis. From the in-game observer perspective at the KSC
  reference frame it appears as one of many 5-6th magnitude
  field stars, indistinguishable from the catalog ~background
  except through the in-game telescope view that resolves the
  amber dust ring.

## Canonical alternatives

The disk origin scenario has two literature-supported readings.
Both are observation-consistent; the cfg picks the more visually
distinctive one as default.

## Open items for follow-up

- **Phase 2 `disk_measurements` ingest.** The DB
  `db/systems/hd_69830.json` does not yet have a
  `disk_measurements` block; Beichman 2005's SED fit (T_dust,
  L_IR/L_★, inner/outer radius) should be ingested as Phase 2
  measurements before the next Phase 3 revisit, mirroring the
  `mass_measurements` / `radius_measurements` schema.
- **Planet b/c/d Phase 3 syntheses.** Completed in the
  disk-followup planet batch. All three Neptune-mass planets
  orbit interior to the warm belt; d at 0.63 AU sits just
  inside the belt's inner shoulder at ~0.9 AU and serves as the
  inner-edge dynamical shepherd via Lisse 2007's 2:1 + 5:2 outer
  MMR coincidence (1.00 / 1.16 AU, both inside the belt). The
  planet-level cfg decisions interact with the warm dust belt
  via this shepherd geometry; future revisits should refine the
  cross-link as more TESS / CHEOPS / JWST data lands.
- **Catastrophic-event hypothesis revisit.** If SPHERE / JWST-MIRI
  / future ALMA imaging resolves the disk geometry — symmetric
  belt vs asymmetric debris cloud, narrow ring vs broader
  distribution — the cfg should re-evaluate the `disk_morphology`
  decision and the canonical-alternatives variant table. As of
  2026 no published resolved imaging exists.
- **Photometrically-accurate disk variant.** The cfg's
  `disk_opacity = 0.25` is a renderer-amplified value;
  a photometrically-accurate variant at opacity ~10⁻³ (rendering
  the dust effectively invisible to the naked eye but observable
  through in-game IR overlays) is listed in Canonical alternatives
  as a candidate cfg variant for future development.
- **X-ray cycle detection campaign.** No published long-baseline
  X-ray monitoring of HD 69830 — a Chandra or XMM-Newton DDT
  proposal could pin the cycle amplitude / period and refine
  the cfg `x_ray_log_lx_cgs_min/max` from tie-break to
  Confidence=high.
- **Spectroscopic detection of dust spectral features.** Beichman
  2005 / Lisse 2007 identify olivine via Spitzer/IRS at 9.7,
  11.3, 23 μm. JWST/MIRI MRS could refine the grain population
  and constrain hot vs cold olivine fractions, feeding back
  into the cfg `disk_tint_rgb_hex` and disk dust temperature.
