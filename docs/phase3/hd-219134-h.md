# HD 219134 h — Phase 3 Synthesis

HD 219134 h is the cold outer gas giant of the bright quiet K3 V dwarf
HD 219134, on a 2247-day (6.15-year) orbit at a = 3.11 AU with a small
eccentricity e = 0.06 (Vogt et al. 2015, Keck/HIRES + APT recommended).
It is RV-only — no transit, no measured radius — and its minimum mass
is Msini = 108.0 ± 6.36 M⊕ = **0.340 M_Jup**, a cold Saturn-class gas
giant. It is by far the coldest planet in the system: the equilibrium
temperature from the project formula T_eq = 278.3 · L^0.25 / √a
(L = 0.264) is just 113 K at zero albedo, comparable to Saturn's
~95 K. At ~3.1 AU the planet sits well beyond the inner super-Earth
family, the long-period anchor of the six-planet system.

**Scenario choice for NearStars: a cold Saturn-class gas giant with an
ammonia-ice cloud deck rendered in warm cream under K3 V illumination,
an unmeasured radius (estimate-only, low confidence), and — as an
optional artistic feature — a Saturn-like icy ring system.** At
0.340 M_Jup and 113 K, h is a cold jovian where ammonia condenses into
a white cloud deck, much like Saturn. The ring is **not observed**
(RV-only, no imaging), but a cold gas giant makes icy rings physically
plausible, so a Saturn-like ring is offered as an artistic cfg option
per the disk-color policy, flagged `ring_observed = false`. No
documented divergence — every value traces to the Vogt 2015 Phase 2
recommended mass, a shown derivation, or an interesting-first tie-break
(the ring being the principal one, flagged as artistic).

## Surface synthesis

HD 219134 h has no solid surface — it is a Saturn-class H₂/He gas giant
at Msini ≈ 0.340 M_Jup. The "surface" relevant for visual rendering is
the cloud deck, conventionally referenced to the 1 bar pressure level
where the optical opacity transitions from clear gas above to dense
particulate-laden gas below.

The defining caveat is that **h does not transit**: its radius is
unmeasured, so radius, gravity, density, and scale height are estimates
flagged at low confidence. The cfg adopts an evolutionary-track
estimate: Saturn itself is 0.83 R_Jup at 0.30 M_Jup, and Fortney 2007
cooling tracks for a 0.34 M_Jup giant at Gyr ages give ~0.9–1.0 R_Jup;
the cfg picks 0.95 R_Jup as the mid-range, giving ~3.8 g⊕ surface
gravity and ~0.55 g/cc bulk density (a low-density cold jovian, less
dense even than Saturn's 0.69 g/cc, as expected for a cooler less-
compressed envelope). The radius could be revised by a future transit
or direct-imaging detection.

The equilibrium temperature is just 113 K (derived) — the coldest in
the system, comparable to Saturn's ~95 K. At this temperature ammonia
condenses into a white cloud deck at the ~0.5–1 bar level, exactly as
on Saturn. The cfg renders h as a cold Saturn analog: a warm-cream
banded jovian. Under the host's 4858 K orange illumination, the
intrinsically near-white ammonia clouds are perceived warmer than the
Sun-illuminated Saturn, giving the warm-cream cloud tint `#f0e6d2`
(a tie-break — no spectrum exists).

## Atmosphere synthesis

The atmospheric inference for h is theoretical (no transit, no
spectrum), but the Saturn-class mass and the 113 K temperature fix it
confidently as a cold jovian.

**Atmosphere present.** At 0.340 M_Jup, h is a hydrogen-helium gas
giant by definition; the bulk H₂/He composition follows from the mass
and the cold equilibrium temperature. The cfg adopts a cold
solar-composition jovian inventory (Lodders 2003 protosolar
abundances): H₂ ~85%, He ~13%, with CH₄, NH₃, and deep H₂O, and the
condensation chemistry truncated at the 113 K equilibrium where
ammonia ices out. This is the canonical cold-Saturn composition (a
tie-break — no measured spectrum).

**Sky appearance.** From above the cloud deck, the sky is a warm-cream
ammonia haze. The host star HD 219134 subtends only ~0.124° at h
(about 1.2× the Sun's apparent size from Earth — modest, because h is
3.1 AU out) and reads warm-orange. The insolation is just 0.027 S⊕, so
the cloud deck is dimly lit; the internal heat flux (~1 W/m², a
tie-break) contributes to the deep convective banding.

## Rotation & spin synthesis

At 3.11 AU on a 6.15-year orbit, h is far too distant for any tidal
interaction — the tidal-damping timescale exceeds the age of the
universe, so h rotates freely and is not locked.

**KSP implementation note.** With no rotation measurement, the cfg
assigns a Saturn-analog ~11-hour rotation period (a tie-break, low
confidence — cold low-mass jovians rotate rapidly, like Saturn's
10.7 h). Obliquity is set to a Saturn-like 26° (interesting-first
tie-break) to give a visually distinctive ring-axis tilt and mild
seasonal banding.

**Mild seasons.** The curated eccentricity is 0.06 — small but
non-zero — giving a peri-to-apo insolation range of S = (1 ± e)⁻² ×
0.027 ≈ 0.024 → 0.030 S⊕ over the 6.15-yr orbit. Combined with the
26° obliquity, this produces a gentle seasonal modulation of the
cloud-band pattern, far milder than the inner planets because the
absolute insolation is so low.

## Visual styling

Combining the atmosphere and ring decisions for a cold Saturn-class
gas giant:

- **Global appearance (orbit view).** A warm-cream banded jovian — a
  cold Saturn analog — with an optional Saturn-like icy ring tilted at
  ~26° to the orbital plane. The ring, if rendered, is the most
  visually distinctive feature in the outer system.
- **Cloud bands.** Warm-cream zonal bands (`#f0e6d2`) with an
  ammonia-ice cloud deck at ~0.5–1 bar and belt/zone contrast (a
  tie-break — no GCM, Saturn-analog band structure).
- **Limb haze.** A warm-cream ammonia haze (`#e8dcc4`) at the limb,
  giving a soft jovian terminator.
- **Saturn-like ring (artistic option, NOT observed).** A single broad
  icy ring from ~0.0008 to ~0.0016 AU (≈ 1.2–2.3 planet radii), ring
  color `#e0d8c8` (icy near-neutral, faintly warmed by the orange host
  light), opacity 0.6 — visibly opaque-to-translucent. This is an
  artistic cfg option: no ring is observed (h is RV-only with no
  imaging), but a cold gas giant makes icy rings physically plausible,
  so the ring is offered per the disk-color policy with
  `ring_observed = false`. The cfg can ship h with or without the ring.
- **Star in sky.** HD 219134 subtends only ~0.124° at h — about 1.2×
  the Sun's angular size from Earth, warm-orange, illuminating the
  cloud deck at just ~0.027× Earth-noon brightness (a dim, cold-jovian
  sky).
- **Sister planets in sky.** All five inner planets (b, c, d, f, g)
  appear interior to h as bright points; the system's transiting pair
  and contested planets are far inside. Near-coplanar geometry.
- **Optional moon system.** A cold Saturn-class giant at 3.1 AU could
  plausibly host a regular satellite system within its Hill sphere
  (the disk-color / ring-candidate note); this is a moon candidate for
  the stability simulator rather than a Phase 3 decision, and is not
  rendered here.

- **Vogt S. S. et al. 2015** — *Six Planets Orbiting HD 219134*, ApJ
  814, 12 (`2015ApJ...814...12V`, doi:10.1088/0004-637X/814/1/12).
  Keck/HIRES + APT solution; the source for h's orbit (P = 2247 d,
  a = 3.11 AU, e = 0.06) and minimum mass (Msini = 108.0 M⊕ =
  0.340 M_Jup, recommended). **The anchor for h's orbit and mass.**
  (h was also detected in Motalebi 2015 as the long-period giant.)
- **Fortney J. J. et al. 2007** — *Planetary radii across five orders
  of magnitude in mass and stellar insolation*, ApJ 659, 1661
  (`2007ApJ...659.1661F`, arXiv:astro-ph/0612671). Evolutionary radius
  tracks; gives the R ≈ 0.9–1.0 R_Jup estimate for a 0.34 M_Jup giant
  at Gyr ages. Drives the (low-confidence) `radius_rjup` estimate.
- **Lodders K. 2003** — *Solar System Abundances and Condensation
  Temperatures of the Elements*, ApJ 591, 1220 (`2003ApJ...591.1220L`).
  Protosolar abundances; sets the cold solar-composition jovian

### Not read — no arXiv preprint or low-priority (~handful)

The h-specific arXiv record is small (RV-only cold giant, no transit or
imaging). The not-read set is dynamical-stability follow-ups (h anchors

evolutionary-model papers that do not change the cold-Saturn rendering
set by the mass and temperature. No ring or moon imaging exists (the
ring is an artistic option, not an observation).

## Open items for follow-up

- **True mass and radius.** h's mass is Msini-only and the radius is an
  evolutionary-track estimate at low confidence. A future astrometric
  inclination (Gaia DR5+, where a 0.34 M_Jup planet at 3.1 AU around a
  nearby star is a strong astrometric target) or direct-imaging
  detection would convert Msini to true mass and constrain the radius.
- **Ring confirmation.** The Saturn-like ring is an artistic option,
  `ring_observed = false`. No imaging exists to confirm or exclude a
  ring. The cfg ships h with the ring as an optional toggle; if a
  future direct-imaging campaign rules it out, the no-ring variant
  becomes the default.
- **Moon system.** A cold Saturn-class giant at 3.1 AU could host a
  regular satellite system; this is a candidate for the stability
  simulator (`phase3/stability-sim/`) rather than a Phase 3 decision.
- **Atmospheric spectrum.** No spectrum exists; a future direct-imaging
  spectrum would replace the tie-break composition and cloud tints
  with measured values.
