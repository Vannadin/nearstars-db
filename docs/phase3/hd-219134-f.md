# HD 219134 f — Phase 3 Synthesis

HD 219134 f is an RV-only, **contested** planet of the bright quiet
K3 V dwarf HD 219134, on a 22.805-day orbit at a = 0.14574 AU. It does
not transit, so the only directly measured physical quantity is the
minimum mass: Msini = 8.9 ± 0.95 M⊕ (Vogt et al. 2015, recommended).
The planet carries a controversy flag (`pl_controv_flag = 1`): its
22.8-day period nearly coincides with one of the candidate stellar
rotation periods (Johnson 2016's 22.83 d Ca II S_HK signal), so part
or all of its RV amplitude could be a stellar-activity artifact rather
than a planet. NearStars keeps f for gameplay variety, flagged as
contested. The equilibrium temperature from the project formula
T_eq = 278.3 · L^0.25 / √a (L = 0.264) is 523 K at zero albedo — a warm
world between the hot transiting pair and the cooler outer planets.

**Scenario choice for NearStars: a warm super-Earth / mini-Neptune
with a modest volatile envelope, hazy warm cloud cover under K3 V
orange illumination, kept-but-flagged as observationally contested, and
with an unmeasured radius (estimate-only, low confidence).** At Msini
≈ 8.9 M⊕ and 523 K, f straddles the super-Earth / sub-Neptune boundary;
the cfg adopts a transitional atmosphere-bearing reading. The radius is
unconstrained (RV-only). No documented divergence — every value traces
to the Vogt 2015 Phase 2 recommended mass, a shown derivation, or an
interesting-first tie-break. The controversy is recorded explicitly in
the Decisions and Open items rather than as a divergence.

## Surface synthesis

HD 219134 f straddles the super-Earth / sub-Neptune boundary. At Msini
≈ 8.9 M⊕ with no measured radius, its bulk nature is ambiguous — it
could be a large rocky super-Earth with a thin atmosphere or a small
sub-Neptune with a modest volatile envelope. The cfg adopts the
transitional, atmosphere-bearing reading: a "surface" referenced to a
cloud deck near the 1 bar level, with the understanding that a rocky
surface may lie beneath if the envelope is thin.

The defining caveat is twofold. First, **f does not transit**: its
radius is unmeasured, so radius, gravity, density, and scale height are
estimates flagged at low confidence. The cfg adopts a mass-radius
estimate at the transition — Zeng 2016's rocky scaling gives ~1.9 R⊕
for 9 M⊕, while a thin envelope (Lopez & Fortney 2014) pushes to
~2.0–2.5 R⊕; the cfg picks 2.0 R⊕ as the mid-range, giving ~2.2 g⊕
surface gravity and ~6.1 g/cc bulk density (consistent with a rocky
core under a thin envelope).

Second, **f is observationally contested**. Its 22.8-day RV period
nearly coincides with the 22.83-day Ca II S_HK rotation signal that
Johnson 2016 measured for the host star — meaning part or all of the RV
amplitude attributed to f could instead be a stellar-activity
modulation at the rotation period. Vogt 2015 flagged this with
`pl_controv_flag = 1`. NearStars keeps f for gameplay variety
(documented here and in Open items) rather than dropping it, but the
contested status is carried explicitly in the Decisions table.

**Color under K3 V illumination.** Under the 4858 K orange host SED,
f's cloud deck and haze are perceived warmer than under Sol. The cfg
renders a warm tan cloud deck (`#e0c098`) with a tan-orange haze limb
(`#d8a878`) — tie-break choices, no spectrum exists.

## Atmosphere synthesis

The atmospheric inference for f is theoretical (no transit, no
spectrum) and complicated by the controversy over the planet's
reality. Assuming f is a genuine planet, the mass and temperature
place it on the super-Earth / sub-Neptune transition.

**Atmosphere present.** At Msini ≈ 9 M⊕ and T_eq ≈ 523 K, f is near
the photoevaporation-valley boundary (Owen & Wu 2017): a planet of
this mass at 0.146 AU around a K dwarf can plausibly retain a thin-to-
moderate volatile envelope. The cfg adopts a metal-enriched H₂/He
envelope with H₂O and CO₂ and a photochemical haze — the canonical
warm transition-object composition. This is a tie-break (no measured
spectrum).

**Sky appearance.** With a modest hazy atmosphere, the sky from the
reference 1 bar level is a warm-tan haze brightening toward the
substellar point. The host star HD 219134 subtends ~2.66° (about 5×
the Sun's apparent size from Earth) and reads warm-orange through the
haze. If the envelope is thin and a rocky surface lies beneath, the
appearance would shade toward a hazy hot super-Earth rather than a
banded sub-Neptune — the cfg leaves this ambiguity to the radius
follow-up.

## Rotation & spin synthesis

At 0.146 AU and 22.805 d, f is far enough out that tidal locking is not
assured. For a ~9 M⊕ volatile planet at this separation around a
0.696 M☉ K dwarf, the tidal-despinning timescale is comparable to the
system age, so the cfg adopts a non-locked, slowly rotating state.

**KSP implementation note.** With no rotation measurement, the cfg
assigns a generic slow rotation period (low confidence, not a measured
value). Obliquity is a small tie-break value.

**No strong seasons.** The curated eccentricity is 0.0 (circularized),
so insolation is steady around the orbit; any banding is driven by the
internal heat flux and the steady 12.4 S⊕ insolation rather than by
seasons.

**Rotation/planet coincidence note.** The very period that makes f a
candidate — 22.8 d — is also the contested stellar rotation period
(Johnson 2016). This is the crux of f's controversy: if the 22.83 d
S_HK signal is the true stellar rotation, an activity-induced RV
modulation at that period mimics a planet. The cfg carries f as a
planet but flags this explicitly.

## Visual styling

Combining the atmosphere decisions for a warm, contested RV-only
transition object:

- **Global appearance (orbit view).** A warm tan, hazy disk —
  intermediate between a rocky super-Earth and a banded sub-Neptune,
  with muted zonal contrast under the orange host light.
- **Cloud deck.** Warm tan cloud cover (`#e0c098`) with an overlying
  photochemical haze; banding muted (tie-break, no GCM).
- **Limb haze.** A tan-orange haze layer (`#d8a878`) scattering the
  orange starlight, giving a soft terminator.
- **Star in sky.** HD 219134 subtends ~2.66° at f — about 5× the Sun's
  angular size from Earth, warm-orange through the haze, illuminating
  the cloud deck at ~12.4× Earth-noon brightness.
- **Sister planets in sky.** The hot transiting pair (b, c) appear
  interior; d, the temperate g, and the cold giant h are exterior. All
  near-coplanar, appearing as bright points.
- **Contested-status rendering note.** f is kept for gameplay variety
  but flagged contested; the rendered planet should be treated as
  provisional, and both its radius (unmeasured) and its very existence
  (activity-mimic risk) are pending observational confirmation.

## Open items for follow-up

- **Confirmation or retraction.** f's 22.8 d period coincides with the
  contested stellar rotation period; a future RV + activity-indicator
  analysis (or a transit detection) would confirm or retract it. If
  retracted, f should be removed; if confirmed, the contested flag is
  cleared. Currently kept for gameplay variety.
- **True mass and radius.** f's mass is Msini-only and the radius is a
  transition-object scaling estimate at low confidence. An inclination
  (astrometry) or transit detection would pin both.
- **Atmospheric spectrum.** No spectrum exists; a future measurement
  would replace the tie-break composition and tints and resolve the
  rocky-vs-sub-Neptune ambiguity.
