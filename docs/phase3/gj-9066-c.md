# GJ 9066 c — Phase 3 Synthesis

GJ 9066 c (= TZ Arietis b in the Quirrenbach et al. 2022 naming) is a
warm, strongly eccentric sub-Saturn-mass giant orbiting the active
M4.5 V flare star GJ 9066 at a = 0.88 ± 0.02 AU with a 771.36 ± 1.34 d
(2.11 yr) period and eccentricity e = 0.46 ± 0.04 (Quirrenbach et al.
2022, CARMENES + HIRES + HARPS RV with a Gaussian-process activity
model). Its minimum mass is M sin i = 0.21 ± 0.02 M_Jup ≈ 67 ± 6 M⊕ —
only ~0.7× Saturn's mass — making it one of only a handful of giant
planets known around a host below 0.3 M☉; Quirrenbach 2022 calls it
"only the second giant planet discovered orbiting a host with mass less
than 0.3 M☉." It was discovered by Feng et al. 2020 and recovered and
refined by Quirrenbach 2022, which simultaneously refuted the
companion 242-day "GJ 9066 b" signal as a stellar-rotation artifact.

The planet is detected by radial velocity only: **its true mass is
unknown** (M sin i lower limit, inclination unconstrained), and there
is no transit, so **no radius is measured**. The strong eccentricity
drives a factor-of-seven insolation swing across the orbit (Quirrenbach
2022: "periodic variations of the insolation by a factor of more than
seven"), from periastron at 0.475 AU to apoastron at 1.285 AU. Even at
periastron the insolation is low — the host is only 0.00254 L☉ — so the
equilibrium temperature stays cold (T_eq ≈ 90 K at periastron, ≈ 55 K at
apoastron, A = 0), well below the warmest gas-giant cloud-chemistry
regimes.

**Scenario choice for NearStars: a cold (T_eq ≈ 55–90 K), eccentric
(e = 0.46) sub-Saturn-mass giant on a 2.1-year orbit, RV-only with true
mass and radius both unconstrained. Visual styling treats it as an
ammonia-cloud-deck sub-Saturn rendered in muted cream under deep-red
M4.5 V illumination, with a low-insolation, weakly-banded appearance and
a strong seasonal insolation swing the only dynamic feature.** The
orbital decisions are anchored on Quirrenbach 2022 (Phase 2 recommended);
mass is the M sin i lower bound; radius and all atmosphere/cloud rows are
tie-break jovian-analog defaults flagged low-confidence, since no
spectrum, no transit, and no thermal map exist.

## Surface synthesis

GJ 9066 c has no solid surface — it is a sub-Saturn-mass H₂/He gas
giant (M sin i ≈ 0.21 M_Jup ≈ 67 M⊕, ~0.7× Saturn). The "surface"
relevant for rendering is the cloud deck, conventionally referenced to
the 1 bar pressure level where the optical opacity transitions from
clear gas above to particulate-laden gas below.

The crucial surface fact is the **low and strongly variable
insolation**. The host is only 0.00254 L☉, so even the time-averaged
insolation at a = 0.88 AU is just S ≈ 0.0033 S⊕ and the equilibrium
temperature is T_eq ≈ 66 K (A = 0). Because the orbit is strongly
eccentric (e = 0.46), the insolation swings by a factor of ~7.3 over
each 2.1-year orbit — from S ≈ 0.011 S⊕ (T_eq ≈ 90 K) at periastron
(0.475 AU) to S ≈ 0.0015 S⊕ (T_eq ≈ 55 K) at apoastron (1.285 AU). This
matches Quirrenbach 2022's note of "periodic variations of the
insolation by a factor of more than seven." Even the periastron peak is
cold by gas-giant standards — colder than Saturn (T_eq ≈ 81 K) for most
of the orbit — so the condensation chemistry is firmly in the
ammonia-ice-cloud-deck regime, with no warm-cloud or alkali-metal
chemistry accessible.

The cfg renders GJ 9066 c as a cold, ammonia-cloud-deck sub-Saturn. The
cloud deck reads as **muted warm cream** (`#d4c8b0`) — ammonia ice is
intrinsically near-white, but under the deep-red 3100 K M4.5 V SED the
perceived hue dims and shifts warm, far less luminous than the
solar-lit cream of Saturn. Belt-zone contrast is muted (`cloud_cover ≈
0.7`, soft banding) because the convective drive at T_eq ≈ 66 K and low
internal heat flux is weak — a softly-banded disk rather than the vivid
banding of an insolation-driven warm jovian. No surface features map to
the interior; the planet is fully fluid down to the H₂/He metallic
transition, with a possible heavy-element core inferred indirectly from
the sub-Saturn mass (cfg encodes no core — no observational constraint).

**The radius is entirely model-inferred.** GJ 9066 c does not transit
(RV-only detection), so no radius is measured. For a ~0.21 M_Jup cold
sub-Saturn, mass-radius relations bracket ~0.8–0.9 R_Jup (Saturn itself
is 0.299 M_Jup at 0.843 R_Jup; Thorngren 2016 cold-giant tracks), and
the cfg picks 0.85 R_Jup (≈ 9.6 R⊕, the DB-stored estimate) at low
confidence. Everything downstream of the radius — surface gravity,
density, scale height — inherits this low-confidence flag.

## Atmosphere synthesis

No transmission or thermal-emission spectrum of GJ 9066 c exists, and
the planet does not transit — the only observational constraints are the
RV orbit and the M sin i lower bound. The atmospheric composition is
therefore drawn entirely from the solar-composition jovian default
(Lodders 2003 protosolar abundance) with Saturn-like volatile
enhancement, condensation chemistry truncated at the cold T_eq ≈ 55–90 K.

**Pressure reference.** Gas giants have no solid surface; the cfg
reference pressure is set at 1 bar (100 000 Pa) near the ammonia
cloud-deck level. Above 1 bar, pressure decreases with scale height
H ≈ 35 km — large despite the low temperature because the sub-Saturn
mass gives a low surface gravity (g ≈ 8.9 m/s² from 0.21 M_Jup,
0.85 R_Jup).

**Composition.** H₂ ≈ 88%, He ≈ 10% by volume — solar protosolar ratios.
Volatiles: CH₄ ≈ 0.3% (Saturn-like, slightly enhanced over protosolar),
NH₃ ≈ 200 ppm (depleted above the cloud deck by condensation at the cold
T_eq), trace H₂O below. These abundances are entirely tie-break defaults
— no spectrum constrains them — but the cfg picks Saturn-analog values
because the sub-Saturn mass and cold equilibrium temperature most closely
resemble Saturn among the Solar System giants.

**Sky appearance.** The cold T_eq and weak convective drive give a
quiescent, low-contrast atmosphere: a thick high-altitude ammonia haze
overlying faintly-banded cloud decks, with little of the vigorous storm
activity of a warm jovian. The most dynamic atmospheric feature is the
**seasonal insolation swing**: over each 2.1-year orbit the top-of-cloud
forcing rises and falls by a factor of ~7, which would drive a modest
seasonal cycle in cloud-deck altitude and haze opacity — the one
time-variable signature on an otherwise static cold giant.

**From a Hill-sphere observer.** From a hypothetical large moon, the
sub-Saturn would dominate the sky as a muted cream banded disk, while
the host star is a tiny deep-red point — only ~0.10° at a = 0.88 AU,
swinging to ~0.18° at periastron and ~0.068° at apoastron — that flares
frequently (the host is a UV-Ceti flare star) and floods the scene with
faint infrared-rich light. The contrast between the pale cream giant and
the small, frequently-flaring red stellar point would be the system's
most distinctive view.

## Rotation & spin synthesis

No direct rotation measurement of GJ 9066 c exists. RV detection
constrains only the orbit, not the planet's spin.

**Tidal damping argument.** At 0.88 AU around a 0.15 M☉ host, the tidal
spin-down timescale for a sub-Saturn-mass giant is far longer than the
age of the universe (Goldreich & Soter 1966 scaling with jovian Q ≈ 10⁵).
GJ 9066 c is **not** tidally locked; the cfg encodes `tidally_locked =
false`. The eccentric orbit (e = 0.46) further argues against
synchronization — pseudo-synchronous rotation at periastron, if anything,
would be faster than the orbital mean motion, not locked to it.

**Rotation period choice.** Among Solar System giants, rotation period
scales weakly with mass: Jupiter (1.0 M_Jup) ≈ 9.93 h, Saturn
(0.3 M_Jup) ≈ 10.7 h, Uranus/Neptune (≈ 0.05 M_Jup) ≈ 16–17 h. For
GJ 9066 c at 0.21 M_Jup — between Saturn and the ice giants in mass — the
cfg picks `rotation_period_hours = 11` as a Saturn-analog value, a
within-window tie-break, not constrained.

**Obliquity.** Tie-break aesthetic: cfg picks 25° (Saturn-analog) for a
visually distinct axis tilt. With the 771-day orbital period and a
moderate obliquity, the obliquity-driven seasons are minor compared to
the eccentricity-driven insolation swing, which dominates the seasonal
forcing.

**KSP implementation note.** Rotation period = 11 h = 39 600 s.
Kopernicus `rotationPeriod = 39600`. Obliquity (axis-tilt cfg) = 25° from
orbital normal. The dominant in-game dynamic for this body is not its
spin but its eccentric orbit — the factor-7 insolation swing over each
2.1-year orbit.

## Visual styling

Combining surface (cloud-deck) and atmosphere decisions:

- **Global appearance.** From orbit, GJ 9066 c reads as a muted cream
  banded sub-Saturn, ~0.85 R_Jup (≈ 9.6 R⊕, ~60 000 km — radius
  model-inferred, not measured). Belt-zone contrast is soft
  (`#d4c8b0` muted cream cloud deck), much less vivid than Jupiter
  because the cold T_eq ≈ 66 K and low insolation give weak convective
  drive. Banding covers ~70% of the disk.
- **Cloud-deck detail.** Ammonia-ice cloud zones appear as smooth, pale
  cream bands with little storm structure — a quiescent cold giant. No
  Great-Red-Spot analog is constrained; cfg renders at most a few faint
  drift vortices as subtle visual interest.
- **Seasonal swing (the dynamic feature).** Over each 2.1-year orbit the
  insolation rises and falls by a factor of ~7 (periastron 0.011 S⊕ →
  apoastron 0.0015 S⊕). A faithful render lets cloud-deck brightness and
  haze opacity breathe slightly with the orbital phase — brightest and
  warmest near periastron, dimmest near apoastron — the one time-variable
  cue on an otherwise static cold disk.
- **Limb haze.** A muted cream limb glow (`#cabfa6`), thin and dim —
  ammonia-deck scattering under deep-red M4.5 V illumination is far
  fainter than Jupiter's solar-lit limb.
- **Nightside.** No direct stellar illumination; the planet's own faint
  internal flux produces only a near-IR thermal glow at ~66 K peak,
  invisible to the eye. Far dimmer than a young or warm jovian.
- **Star in sky.** GJ 9066 subtends only ~0.10° from the planet at
  a = 0.88 AU — about 1/5 the Sun's angular size from Earth — swinging
  to ~0.18° at periastron and ~0.068° at apoastron. It appears as a
  small, deep red-orange (M4.5 V) point that **flares frequently** (the
  host is a UV-Ceti eruptive flare star), the most animated element of
  the sky.
- **Ring system.** No ring (`ring_present = false`) — a tie-break, since
  RV-only detection gives no imaging either way. A Saturn-like ring is
  preserved as a cfg variant (Open items) for users who prefer it.
- **Sister planets in sky.** None — GJ 9066 c is the only confirmed
  planet; the Feng 2020 "GJ 9066 b" 242-day candidate was refuted
  (Quirrenbach 2022).

## Open items for follow-up

- **True mass — inclination is unconstrained.** GJ 9066 c is RV-only, so
  only M sin i ≈ 0.21 M_Jup is bounded; the true mass could be larger if
  the orbit is inclined. Quirrenbach 2022 notes the close (4.47 pc) host
  and large angular semi-major axis (~0.27″) make GJ 9066 c a favorable
  future direct-imaging target, and a Gaia astrometric acceleration search
  is the most sensitive near-term test of inclination / a third body. A
  measured inclination would convert M sin i to a true mass and tighten
  every mass-derived row.
- **Radius is model-inferred, not measured.** No transit; the 0.85 R_Jup
  (≈ 9.6 R⊕) radius comes from cold-sub-Saturn mass-radius relations
  (Thorngren 2016). A future direct-imaging or (unlikely) transit
  detection would replace it and lift the density / gravity / scale-height
  rows from low confidence.
- **Atmosphere spectrum.** All composition, cloud, and haze rows are
  tie-break jovian-analog defaults. A future direct-imaging spectrum
  (the favorable contrast Quirrenbach 2022 highlights) could yield CH₄ /
  NH₃ abundances and constrain the cloud-deck altitude, replacing the
  Decisions rows `atmosphere_composition`, `cloud_morphology`, and
  `bond_albedo`.
- **Eccentricity-driven seasonal rendering.** The factor-7 insolation
  swing (periastron → apoastron) is the planet's defining dynamic feature.
  A future cfg revision could phase-lock cloud-deck brightness and haze
  opacity to the orbital phase so the player sees the giant "breathe" over
  each 2.1-year orbit.
- **Ring tie-break preservation.** cfg picks `ring_present = false`, but
  RV-only detection gives no imaging constraint either way. A Saturn-like
  ring system is preserved here as the alternative cfg variant for users
  who prefer it.
